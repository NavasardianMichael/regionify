## Publish checklist (in order)

- [ ] **Record your demo video** first — 30-60 seconds, portrait 9:16 or square 1:1 (LinkedIn's algorithm favours native video in vertical/square ratios). See asset brief in `post.md`.
- [ ] **Capture supporting screenshots** — 3-5 stills of the app (see asset briefs in `post.md`). PNG, 1080-2160 px on the long edge.
- [ ] **Pick a hook** from the variants in `post.md`. First 2 lines are 90% of the game.
- [ ] **Post Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday** — never Friday-Monday.
- [ ] **Post at 4-6 PM UTC+4** (= 8-10 AM US Eastern = 2-4 PM Central European) — hits US morning + EU afternoon in one window.
- [ ] **Upload the video as native LinkedIn media** — do NOT link to YouTube in the post body.
- [ ] **Paste the body from `post.md`** as-is (after picking a hook).
- [ ] **Publish**, then within 30 seconds paste the first comment with the regionify.pro link (see `post.md`).
- [ ] **Block the next 3 hours** for real-time replies. First-hour engagement decides total reach.

---

## The two algorithm rules that matter most (2026)

### 1. No external links in the body

LinkedIn actively suppresses reach on posts containing URLs to external sites — often by 5-10×. The link to regionify.pro **always goes in the first comment**, posted by you within 30 seconds of publishing. In the body, refer to it as "link in the first comment."

### 2. First 2 lines are the whole post

On mobile, LinkedIn cuts the post after ~140 characters with a "see more" fold. If those first 2 lines don't earn the tap, nothing else in the post matters. Pick the strongest hook variant from `post.md` and test-preview on mobile before publishing.

## Hashtags

**3-5 hashtags at the bottom** of the post. Not at the top. Not more than 5.

Recommended pool (mix one broad, 2-3 niche):

- Broad: `#SaaS` `#Startup` `#DataVisualization` `#DataViz`
- Niche: `#ChoroplethMap` `#GeoData` `#BuildInPublic` `#IndieHacker` `#SoloFounder`
- Vertical (pick one if it fits your audience): `#MarketResearch` `#PoliticalAnalytics` `#JournalismTools`

Never use a branded hashtag on your own posts (`#Regionify`) — LinkedIn treats repeated self-tagging as spam signal.

## What NOT to do

- No "excited to announce" / "thrilled to share" openers — LinkedIn readers auto-scroll.
- No external URLs in the main post body.
- No image AND external link in the same post — LinkedIn cuts reach on the combo.
- No walls of hashtags at the top.
- No "DM me for details" — give readers a public path.
- No engagement-bait polls or fake-humble openers.
